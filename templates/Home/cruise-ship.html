Eagleaviation Diploma Course in Cruise Ship

Throughout this program, we'll provide you an interesting historical view at the talents and qualifications wanted to function in the cruise line industry, the application process, and, as a consequence, the lifestyle of a cruising member of the crew. This course equips prospective crew members and novices to the profession with the requisite experience to embark on this career aboard cargo haulers. Educators who continue on to the Cruise Ship Management Diploma course will not only develop a greater understanding of the economics and marketing of underwater cruise ships and the operations and maintenance challenges they face, but will also be able to tackle more sophisticated difficulties such as people management, organisational culture, analysis techniques, and etc.

The two-year curriculum is designed to provide students with the information and knowledge required for a professional future in the cruise industry's hospitality businesses, as well as a working knowledge of work life on a cruise ship. Learners will be able about front-office operations, catering services, sanitation, client service, efficient leadership, critical Computer capabilities, and finance as section of the training.

They will also be introduced to the larger universe of the cruise lines, including its background, competitors, consumers, laws, and safety and security considerations. This curriculum was developed with the cooperation of cruise industry specialists, ensuring that students would be adequately equipped for their professional prospects. During the first year, candidates must pass 5 months internship in a 5-star hotel as part of their programme of study. They can apply to work on a cruise ship in their second year.
